Reaserch Methods and Professional Practice Module

Objectives:.

  • Appraise the professional, legal, social, cultural and ethical issues that affect computing professionals.
  • Appraise the principles of academic investigation, applying them to a research topic in the applicable computing field.
  • Evaluate critically existing literature, research design and methodology for the chosen topic, including data analysis processes.
  • Produce and evaluate critically a research proposal for the chosen topic.

▼ Unit 1 ▼

Topic:

  • Introduction to Research Methods. The Scientific Investigation and Ethics in Computing.

Objectives

  • Understanding why ethics are important and how they may relate to an area of research and a professional practice.
  • Differentiating between inductive and deductive reasoning.

Outcomes

  • Learned the difference between deduction and induction in inferences.
  • Explored research ethics.
  • For the literature review, I have chosen the implement machine learning tools and/or techniques in crime prediction. From all the available topics I have found this the most interesting due to the recent rise in crimes accross the globe.

▼ Unit 2 ▼

Topic:

  • Research Questions, the Literature Review and the Research Proposal

Objectives

  • Examining the characteristics that make up a suitable research topic.
  • Exploring rational and creative methods for formulating a research idea.
  • Identifying means of transforming research ideas into crafted research questions and proposal.

Outcomes

  • Followed the literature review guide questions to support the planning, revising and refining of the first summative assessment.
  • Explored the importance of research questions as well as the methods to develop them and constructing a research proposal.
  • Introduced to the concepts of literature reviews, their purpose and techniques to evaluate them.

Objectives

  • Interviews, Survey Methods, and Questionnaire Design

Objectives

  • Understanding how interviews and surveys fit into research and whether either or both might be useful for an investigation.
  • Understanding how pre- and post-testing can be used in research.
  • Designing a questionnaire to obtain the responses you will need for your investigation.
  • Understanding how to analyse the data obtained.

Outcomes

  • Explored new concepts such as pre- and post-testing.
  • Understood the different types of research questions and which one fit best in which scenario.
